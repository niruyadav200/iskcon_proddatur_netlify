Our Mission

ISKCON Proddatur is dedicated to spreading Krishna Consciousness through the teachings of Bhagavad Gita and the practice of bhakti yoga. We aim to create a spiritual oasis where devotees can chant, dance, feast, and experience the divine presence of Lord Krishna. Join us in our journey of self-realization and spiritual awakening.

ISKCON Proddatur - A Temple of Peace and Devotion

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) was founded by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada in 1966. ISKCON Proddatur continues this mission of spiritual enlightenment.

  • Daily Spiritual Programs

    Join us for morning and evening arati, Bhagavad Gita classes, and devotional kirtan sessions

  • Community & Fellowship

    Be part of a loving spiritual community dedicated to serving Krishna and humanity

At ISKCON Proddatur, we welcome everyone to experience the joy of Krishna consciousness through chanting, dancing, feasting on sanctified vegetarian food (prasadam), and learning the ancient wisdom of the Vedas. Our temple is a sanctuary where spiritual seekers can find peace, purpose, and divine connection.

Temple Services

Discover the various spiritual services and programs offered at ISKCON Proddatur

Daily Darshan

Experience the divine darshan of Sri Sri Radha Krishna with morning and evening arati ceremonies. Temple opens at 4:30 AM for Mangala Arati.

Festivals & Events

Celebrate major festivals including Janmashtami, Radhashtami, Gaura Purnima, and more with grand celebrations and spiritual programs.

Prasadam Distribution

Enjoy sumptuous Krishna prasadam prepared with love and devotion. Free prasadam distribution on Sundays and special occasions.

Bhagavad Gita Classes

Learn the ancient wisdom of Bhagavad Gita through regular classes, seminars, and personal guidance from experienced devotees.

Kirtan & Bhajan

Join our daily kirtan sessions and experience the transcendental vibration of the Hare Krishna maha-mantra and devotional songs.

Volunteer Programs

Serve the Lord through various volunteer opportunities including deity worship, prasadam seva, book distribution, and temple maintenance.
